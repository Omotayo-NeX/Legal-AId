Intellectual Property Basics for Nigerian Creators: Protect Your Work

IP Rights for Nigerian Creators

What is Intellectual Property?

Intellectual Property (IP) refers to creations of the mind - inventions, artistic works, designs, symbols, names, and images used in commerce. IP law gives you legal ownership and control over your creative work.

üéØ Why IP Matters

Protecting your intellectual property allows you to profit from your creativity, prevents others from stealing your work, and builds the value of your brand or business.

Types of Intellectual Property in Nigeria

1. Copyright

Protects original creative works including:

  • Literary works (books, articles, software code)
  • Musical works and sound recordings
  • Artistic works (paintings, photographs, graphics)
  • Films and videos
  • Dramatic works

2. Trademarks

Protects brand identifiers including:

  • Business names and logos
  • Product names
  • Slogans and taglines
  • Distinctive packaging

3. Patents

Protects inventions and innovations including:

  • New products or processes
  • Technical innovations
  • Manufacturing methods

4. Industrial Designs

Protects the visual design of objects - shape, configuration, pattern, or ornamentation.

Copyright in Nigeria

Governed by the Copyright Act 2022 (which replaced the 1988 Act), Nigerian copyright law provides automatic protection.

Key Facts:

  • Automatic Protection: Copyright exists automatically when you create an original work - no registration required
  • Duration: Life of the author + 70 years (for most works)
  • Rights Granted: Exclusive right to reproduce, distribute, perform, display, and create derivative works
  • Enforcement: Nigerian Copyright Commission (NCC) enforces copyright law

What Copyright Does NOT Protect:

  • Ideas (only the expression of ideas)
  • Facts and data
  • Titles and short phrases
  • Methods or procedures

How to Protect Your Copyright:

1. Mark Your Work: Use ¬© symbol with year and your name (e.g., ¬© 2025 Your Name)

2. Keep Records: Document creation dates, drafts, and evidence of authorship

3. Register (Optional): While not required, you can register with NCC for stronger evidence of ownership

4. Use Contracts: When sharing work, use Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) and licensing agreements

Trademarks in Nigeria

Administered by the Trademarks, Patents and Designs Registry under the Federal Ministry of Industry, Trade and Investment.

Why Register a Trademark?

  • Exclusive rights to use the mark for your goods/services
  • Legal protection against copycats and counterfeiters
  • Brand value - trademarks are business assets
  • Nationwide protection in Nigeria

Trademark Registration Process:

Step Action
1. Search Check if your mark is available (not already registered)
2. Application File TM2 form with the Registry, specify goods/services class
3. Examination Registry examines application (4-6 months)
4. Publication Published in Trademarks Journal for opposition (60 days)
5. Registration If no opposition, trademark is registered

Patents in Nigeria

Patents protect new inventions - products, processes, or technical solutions to problems.

Requirements for Patentability:

  • Novelty: The invention must be new (not previously disclosed)
  • Inventive Step: Non-obvious to someone skilled in the field
  • Industrial Applicability: Can be made or used in industry

What Cannot Be Patented:

  • Scientific theories and mathematical methods
  • Literary, dramatic, musical or artistic works
  • Business methods or computer programs (as such)
  • Medical treatment methods

Patent Protection:

  • Duration: 20 years from filing date
  • Rights: Exclusive right to make, use, sell the invention
  • Cost: ‚Ç¶30,000 - ‚Ç¶100,000+ (filing fees, plus legal/agent fees)

üí° Before Filing a Patent

DO NOT publicly disclose your invention before filing. Public disclosure can destroy novelty and make your invention unpatentable.

Copyright Infringement: What to Do

If Someone Steals Your Work:

Step 1: Document the Infringement
Take screenshots, save copies, note dates and URLs

Step 2: Send Cease and Desist
Formal letter demanding they stop using your work

Step 3: Report to Platform
Use DMCA takedown for websites, social media platforms

Step 4: Contact NCC
Nigerian Copyright Commission investigates infringement

Step 5: Legal Action
File lawsuit for damages and injunction

Remedies Available:

  • Injunction (court order to stop infringement)
  • Damages (monetary compensation)
  • Account of profits (infringer pays profits made from your work)
  • Seizure and destruction of infringing copies

Common Questions

Do I need to register copyright in Nigeria?

No, copyright protection is automatic upon creation. However, registration provides stronger evidence of ownership in disputes.

Can I trademark my business name?

Yes, but business name registration with CAC is different from trademark registration. For full protection, register both.

Can I use someone else's copyrighted work?

Only with permission (license) or if it qualifies as "fair dealing" (limited use for criticism, research, news reporting, or education).

Practical Tips for Creators

For Musicians & Artists:

  • Register works with NCC and collect societies (COSON, MCSN)
  • Use written contracts for collaborations
  • Watermark digital artwork before sharing online
  • Keep original files and creation records

For Business Owners:

  • Register your brand name and logo as trademarks
  • Use NDAs when sharing business plans with investors
  • Include IP clauses in employee contracts
  • Monitor the market for copycats and counterfeits

For Software Developers:

  • Copyright protects code - document authorship
  • Use licenses (MIT, GPL, etc.) when sharing code
  • Consider trade secret protection for proprietary algorithms
  • Register business software patents where applicable
