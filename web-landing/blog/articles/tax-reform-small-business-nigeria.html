A comprehensive, practical guide for Nigerian entrepreneurs and SME owners on navigating the tax reforms taking effect January 1, 2026.

While the reforms bring complexity, they also offer substantial opportunities for cost savings and simplified compliance - if you know how to leverage them.

This guide cuts through the legal jargon to provide actionable insights specifically for small business owners, sole proprietors, and entrepreneurs.

Capital Gains Tax: What Changed and Why It Matters

One of the most significant changes for businesses is the dramatic increase in Capital Gains Tax (CGT).

The Big Jump

  • Old Rate: 10%
  • New Rate (from Jan 2026): 30%

This 200% increase means selling business assets, property, or investments will trigger substantially higher tax liability.

Newly Taxable Assets

CGT now applies to assets that were previously untaxed:

  • Cryptocurrencies: If your business holds Bitcoin, Ethereum, or other crypto, selling them now triggers CGT
  • Intellectual Property: Selling patents, trademarks, or copyrights
  • Forex Gains: Gains from foreign exchange transactions
  • Digital Assets: Domain names, software licenses, digital content rights

VAT Changes: Better Input Credit Claims

The VAT rate remains at 7.5%, but how you can claim input VAT has improved significantly.

What's New with VAT?

Under the old system, many businesses struggled to claim VAT on certain purchases. The new law expands what you can claim:

‚úÖ Previously: Limited Claims

Many businesses could only claim input VAT on goods, not services or capital assets.

‚úÖ Now: Comprehensive Claims

You can claim input VAT on ALL business purchases:

  • Goods (as before)
  • Services (NEW)
  • Fixed assets like equipment and machinery (NEW)

Payroll and Employee Tax Implications

The tax reform's changes to personal income tax will affect your payroll administration.

Tax Exemption for Low-Income Employees

Employees earning ‚Ç¶800,000 or less annually are now fully exempt from income tax. This means:

  • Reduced Payroll Admin: No need to calculate, withhold, or remit PAYE for minimum wage workers
  • Employee Benefit: Lower-paid workers take home more (good for morale and retention)
  • Employer Relief: Less compliance burden and lower risk of penalties for small errors

Action Item for Employers

  1. Review your payroll to identify employees earning ‚â§ ‚Ç¶800,000 annually
  2. Update payroll software to exclude these employees from PAYE from January 2026
  3. Communicate the change to affected employees (they'll be happy about take-home pay increase!)
  4. Ensure you're NOT withholding tax unnecessarily (it's illegal and creates liabilities)

New Tax Incentive: Economic Development Tax Incentive (EDTI)

The tax reform replaces the old Pioneer Status Incentive with a new framework designed to be more targeted and performance-based.

How EDTI Works

  • Credit-Based: Tax relief tied to actual investment amounts
  • Sector-Specific: Focused on priority industries aligned with national development goals
  • Performance Requirements: Must meet milestones to retain incentives
  • Time-Limited: Incentives for specific periods with renewal based on performance

Priority Sectors Likely to Benefit

  • Agriculture and agro-processing
  • Manufacturing
  • Technology and innovation
  • Infrastructure development
  • Renewable energy
  • Export-oriented businesses

If your business operates in these sectors and plans significant investments, explore EDTI opportunities with a tax consultant.

Digital Compliance: What the NRS Means for You

The transformation of FIRS into the Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS) brings a digital-first approach to tax administration.

What to Expect

  • Online Filing: Most tax returns will be filed electronically
  • Digital Payments: Tax payments through digital channels
  • Automated Processing: Faster processing of refunds and queries
  • Real-Time Tracking: Monitor your tax status and compliance online
  • Reduced Physical Visits: Less need to visit tax offices in person

Prepare Your Business

  1. Digital Infrastructure: Ensure reliable internet and basic computer literacy for your finance team
  2. Email Account: Maintain an active, monitored business email for NRS communications
  3. Record Keeping: Digitize financial records for easy upload and retrieval
  4. Tax Software: Consider cloud-based accounting software that integrates with tax filing

Frequently Asked Questions for Small Businesses

I operate as a sole proprietor, not a registered company. Do these changes affect me?

Yes, but differently. As a sole proprietor, you pay personal income tax, not corporate tax. You'll benefit from the ‚Ç¶800K exemption (if your income is that low) and progressive tax bands. You're not affected by the small company definition since that only applies to incorporated businesses.

My consulting business earns ‚Ç¶25 million annually. Can I qualify as a small company?

No. The law explicitly excludes professional services businesses from small company status, regardless of revenue. Consulting is a professional service, so you'll be taxed at regular corporate rates even though your revenue is below ‚Ç¶50 million.

What happens if my business grows and exceeds the ‚Ç¶50 million threshold during the year?

If you exceed the threshold mid-year, you'll likely lose small company status for that entire tax year. Plan accordingly - if you're close to the threshold, consider whether it's worth deferring some revenue to maintain preferential status (consult a tax advisor on this).

Can I claim VAT on services I purchased before January 2026?

Generally, no. VAT input claims usually apply to the tax period in which the purchase was made. Services purchased in 2025 would follow 2025 rules. However, seek professional advice on transition rules.

My employees earn below minimum wage. Are they automatically exempt from tax?

Employees earning ‚Ç¶800,000 or less annually are exempt. Minimum wage is a separate labor law concept. Focus on the ‚Ç¶800K annual income threshold for tax purposes.
